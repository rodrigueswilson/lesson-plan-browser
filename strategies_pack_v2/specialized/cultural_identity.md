### Strategy ID: culturally_responsive_pedagogy

**Name:** Culturally Responsive Pedagogy

**Aliases:** Culturally sustaining pedagogy, Asset-based teaching

**Present In:** v1.4 enhancement

**Evidence Tier:** research-backed

**High-Level Categories:** Cultural & Identity Affirming

**Primary Skill:** integrated

**Secondary Skills:** speaking, reading, writing, listening

**Skill Weights:** speaking: 0.3; listening: 0.2; reading: 0.25; writing: 0.25

**Delivery Mode:** push-in, whole-group, small-group

**L1 Mode:** translanguaging, preview-review

**Integrated Framework:** Yes

**Core Principle:**
Center students' cultural knowledge, experiences, and ways of knowing in curriculum and instruction.

**Applicable Contexts:**
- All subject areas
- Community connections
- Identity affirmation activities

**Implementation Steps:**
1. Learn about students' cultural backgrounds and community knowledge.
2. Integrate cultural references, examples, and perspectives into curriculum.
3. Create opportunities for students to share and build on cultural knowledge.

**Primary Benefits:**
- Increases engagement and academic achievement
- Affirms cultural identity and builds confidence

**Research Foundation:** Geneva Gay, Gloria Ladson-Billings, Django Paris

**Cross-References:** funds_of_knowledge, heritage_language_connections, interactive_read_alouds

**Short Definition:**
Teaching approach that centers students' cultural knowledge, experiences, and ways of knowing in curriculum and instruction.

**Long Definition:**
Culturally Responsive Pedagogy is a teaching approach that recognizes, honors, and builds upon students' cultural knowledge, experiences, and ways of knowing as foundations for learning. Teachers learn about students' cultural backgrounds, community knowledge systems, and family traditions, then integrate these cultural references, examples, and perspectives into curriculum and instruction. This approach goes beyond surface-level cultural celebrations to deeply embed cultural ways of knowing into academic content. Students see their cultures reflected and valued in classroom materials, discussions, and activities. The pedagogy creates bridges between home and school knowledge while maintaining high academic expectations. Implementation requires ongoing cultural learning by teachers, authentic community connections, and systematic integration of cultural knowledge into lesson planning and assessment practices.

**Look-Fors (Observable Indicators):**
- Integration of students' cultural knowledge and experiences into curriculum
- Authentic cultural references and perspectives in instructional materials
- Opportunities for students to share and build on cultural knowledge
- Teacher learning about and honoring students' cultural backgrounds

**Non-Examples (Avoid):**
- Superficial cultural celebrations without deeper curriculum integration
- Stereotypical or tokenistic cultural references
- Deficit-based approaches that view culture as obstacle to learning

### Strategy ID: funds_of_knowledge

**Name:** Funds of Knowledge Approach

**Aliases:** Community cultural wealth, Asset-based family engagement

**Present In:** v1.4 enhancement

**Evidence Tier:** research-backed

**High-Level Categories:** Cultural & Identity Affirming

**Primary Skill:** integrated

**Secondary Skills:** speaking, listening, reading, writing

**Skill Weights:** speaking: 0.4; listening: 0.3; reading: 0.15; writing: 0.15

**Delivery Mode:** push-in, whole-group, small-group

**L1 Mode:** translanguaging, preview-review

**Integrated Framework:** Yes

**Core Principle:**
Identify and leverage the knowledge, skills, and practices that students bring from their families and communities.

**Applicable Contexts:**
- Family engagement activities
- Project-based learning
- Community connections

**Implementation Steps:**
1. Conduct home visits or family interviews to learn about household knowledge.
2. Map connections between family knowledge and academic curriculum.
3. Create learning opportunities that build on community expertise.

**Primary Benefits:**
- Validates family knowledge and strengthens home-school connections
- Provides authentic contexts for academic learning

**Research Foundation:** Luis Moll, Norma González, Cathy Amanti

**Cross-References:** culturally_responsive_pedagogy, heritage_language_connections, project_based_learning

**Short Definition:**
Educational approach that identifies and leverages the knowledge, skills, and practices students bring from families and communities.

**Long Definition:**
Funds of Knowledge Approach is an educational framework that systematically identifies and leverages the knowledge, skills, and cultural practices that students bring from their families and communities into classroom learning. Teachers engage in ethnographic research through home visits, family interviews, and community exploration to understand the rich knowledge systems present in students' households. This knowledge includes practical skills, cultural traditions, work experiences, and community expertise that can be connected to academic curriculum. Teachers then design learning experiences that build on this community knowledge, creating authentic contexts for academic content while validating family expertise. The approach transforms deficit-based views of families into asset-based recognition of cultural wealth. Implementation requires genuine relationship-building with families, systematic documentation of community knowledge, and creative curriculum connections.

**Look-Fors (Observable Indicators):**
- Systematic learning about family and community knowledge systems
- Curriculum connections between household knowledge and academic content
- Family expertise integrated into classroom learning experiences
- Asset-based view of families as knowledge holders and contributors

**Non-Examples (Avoid):**
- Deficit-based assumptions about family knowledge or capabilities
- Surface-level family engagement without deeper knowledge exploration
- Curriculum that ignores or devalues community knowledge systems
