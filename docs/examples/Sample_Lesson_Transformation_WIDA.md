## **WIDA-Enhanced Bilingual Transformation**

### **Pre-Analysis:**
- **Grade Cluster:** 4-5 (Expanding academic language and content integration)
- **Expected Proficiency Range:** Levels 2-5 (typical for 5th grade ELLs)
- **Key Language Use:** **Inform** (providing factual information, describing plant structures)
- **Language Dimension Focus:** **Word/Phrase** (precision of scientific vocabulary)
- **Content Vocabulary:** roots, stem, leaves, flowers, nutrients, photosynthesis, transport

## **Selected Strategies Summary**

### **Strategy 1: graphic_organizers**
- **WIDA Alignment:**
  - **Key Language Use:** Inform (organizing factual information about plant structures)
  - **Primary Dimension:** Discourse (organization of scientific information)
  - **Grade Cluster Focus:** Academic content organization with vocabulary for 4-5 cluster
- **Proficiency Adaptations:**
  - **Levels 1-2:** Picture-based plant web with minimal text, Portuguese labels allowed
  - **Levels 3-4:** Bilingual comparison charts with sentence frames for plant functions
  - **Levels 5-6:** Complex scientific organizers analyzing structure-function relationships
- **Research Foundation:** Marzano, Pickering & Pollock (visual representation enhances comprehension)
- **Cross-References:** SIOP (visual supports), Think-Pair-Share (discussion structure)

### **Strategy 2: think_pair_share**
- **WIDA Alignment:**
  - **Key Language Use:** Inform (sharing factual observations about plants)
  - **Primary Dimension:** Discourse (structured sharing of scientific observations)
  - **Grade Cluster Focus:** Structured discussions with academic vocabulary for 4-5 cluster
- **Proficiency Adaptations:**
  - **Levels 1-2:** Think with visuals, pair with bilingual support, share single words/gestures
  - **Levels 3-4:** Structured frames for thinking and sharing: "Eu observo / I observe"
  - **Levels 5-6:** Academic discourse about plant adaptations and functions
- **Research Foundation:** Frank Lyman, Spencer Kagan (structured interaction increases participation)
- **Cross-References:** Translanguaging (L1 support), Graphic Organizers (visual thinking)

### **Strategy 3: siop**
- **WIDA Alignment:**
  - **Key Language Use:** Inform (content and language integration for plant science)
  - **Primary Dimension:** All three (comprehensive language development)
  - **Grade Cluster Focus:** Academic language development with science content for 4-5 cluster
- **Proficiency Adaptations:**
  - **Levels 1-2:** Heavy visual supports, hands-on exploration, minimal text
  - **Levels 3-4:** Graphic organizers, sentence frames, structured academic talk
  - **Levels 5-6:** Independent research, academic discourse, specialized vocabulary
- **Research Foundation:** Echevarria, Vogt & Short (simultaneous content and language development)
- **Cross-References:** All strategies integrate within SIOP framework

## **Key WIDA Enhancements Demonstrated**

1. **Proficiency Differentiation:** Every activity includes specific adaptations for Levels 1-2, 3-4, and 5-6
2. **Asset-Based Language:** Emphasizes what students CAN do at each level
3. **Grade-Cluster Alignment:** All activities appropriate for 4-5 developmental expectations
4. **Key Language Use Integration:** Consistent focus on "Inform" throughout lesson
5. **Language Dimension Targeting:** Emphasis on Word/Phrase precision for scientific vocabulary
6. **Cultural Integration:** Portuguese cognates and family knowledge validation
7. **Assessment Differentiation:** Multiple ways to demonstrate understanding
8. **Research-Based Implementation:** Every strategy cites specific WIDA adaptations
